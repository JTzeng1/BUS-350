Student Loans: What You Should Know

Everything You Need to Know About Student Loans

If you're considering student loans to help pay for your education, you're not alone. But the more money you borrow now, the more you’ll have to spend on monthly payments after you graduate.

Before Borrowing

Make sure you fill out the FAFSA form as soon as it opens on October 1. Minimize how much you borrow by cutting costs, applying for grants and scholarships, and considering other options like working part-time or setting up a tuition installment plan.

Choosing the Right Student Loan

1. Learn About Different Loan Types

Most students have two main options: federal loans or private loans. Explore federal loans first before looking at private options. The main types are:

  • Direct Subsidized: For undergraduates; interest is paid by the government while in school.
  • Direct Unsubsidized: For undergraduate/graduate students; interest accrues while in school.
  • Direct PLUS: For parents of undergraduates or graduate students; requires a credit check.
  • Private Loans: From banks or credit unions; often requires a co-signer.
2. Explore Federal Options First

Federal loans usually cost less, offer fixed interest rates, and have flexible repayment options. Advantages include:

  • No credit check (except Parent PLUS loans)
  • Lower interest rates than most private loans
  • Fixed interest rates
  • Flexible repayment options and protections

Steps to get a federal loan:

  • Submit FAFSA.
  • Follow your school’s financial aid instructions.
  • Complete entrance counseling and sign a Master Promissory Note.
3. Private Loans

Consider private loans only if federal options aren’t enough. Private loans usually cost more and have fewer repayment options. Tips:

  • Talk to your school’s financial aid office for certification.
  • Line up a co-signer if required.
  • Shop around for low interest rates and flexible repayment options.
  • Avoid using credit cards to finance school; interest rates are higher and repayment is less flexible.
