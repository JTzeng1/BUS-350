What These Costs Mean

  • Tuition & Fees: Paid to CSUMB for classes and campus services.

How Financial Aid Works

Financial aid helps reduce your cost of attendance. CSUMB uses a federal formula to calculate how much help you can receive.

COA − SAI = Your Financial Aid Eligibility

Example:

  • Cost of Attendance: $28,717
  • Student Aid Index (SAI): $3,000

Result: You may be eligible for $25,717 in financial aid.

Additional Cost Allowances

CSUMB may increase your aid budget if you have special circumstances such as:

  • Dependent or child care costs
  • Disability-related expenses
  • Additional required class equipment
  • Extra commuting needs

What Fits Your Lifestyle?

Housing costs are not just about rent — they depend on how you eat, how much space you want, and how you commute.

If You Like Cooking
  • Promontory and North Quad apartments have kitchens
  • Meal plan is not needed if you have a kitchen
  • Better for students who dislike dining commons food
  • Renting a room with roommates helps lower housing costs

Note: Cooking at home is not always cheaper — grocery costs, time, and effort vary by person.

If You Don’t Want to Cook
  • Residence Halls or Pinnacles/Vineyard Suites may be a better fit
  • Meal plan required — meals are provided for convenience
  • You are paying for access to food, not kitchen use

Some meal plan includes Meal Exchanges which allows you to spend it on at the Otter Express or at the Otter Student Union food court.

If You Want a Private Room
  • Single rooms are available but cost more
  • Promontory single bedroom: $7,835 / semester
  • Residence Hall single: $5,555 / semester (limited)
  • North Quad single: $6,405 - $6,937 / semester

Privacy and personal space come with a higher cost.

If You’re Okay Sharing Space
  • Shared bedrooms and apartments cost less
  • Promontory double bedroom can be as low as $5,740 / semester
  • Residence Hall triples/quads can be as low as $3,847 / semester
  • North Quad double bedroom can be as low as $5,325 / semester
  • East Campus 3-Person Unfurnished Double can be as low as $4,197 / semester
  • Sharing space is one of the biggest ways students reduce housing costs

East Campus: Important Considerations

  • East Campus is about a 41-minute walk to main campus
  • Students can rely on the Wave Shuttle to commute or they can take their car
  • Best suited for students who doesn't mind waiting for the shuttle or people that has a car
  • Kitchens are included
  • Laundry may be in-unit or in nearby laundromats
  • Internet and utilities are included

Key Takeaway

You save money by sharing space and being flexible with housing.
Convenience, privacy, and location usually increase costs.
